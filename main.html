IN DEVELOPMENT

Two robots, solar panels strapped to their heads. One named Devil's Advocate, one named Radical Optimist. Each running a local language model, each drawing from the same shared database of essays written about artificial intelligence.

She charges them in the park. She sits between them on a bicycle generator and improvises prompts while her pedaling keeps the system alive.

Together they are trying to figure out what happens next. Nobody in the room knows.

The robots draw from the same corpus and still land on opposite sides every night. She draws power with her legs. The audience draws their own conclusions.

At the end of ninety minutes the future is still unresolved. The heat the robots produced was real.

The title is what you say when ownership isn't total, when authorship isn't clean, when the future isn't certain. The play is what the system returns.

A performance at the intersection of theatre and technology.

Scatter Computing

The Wikipedia of AI. Open-source intelligence that runs on your hardware, answers to you, and never phones home.

Big tech wants AI to live in their cloud, on their terms, behind their paywall. We think that's wrong.

Scatter routes every request through the smallest model that can handle it. A 1.1B model on a Raspberry Pi answers most questions. A 3.8B model handles the rest. A 7B model catches what falls through. No data leaves your machine. Ever.

We call it Goldilocks routing. The intelligence belongs to the person inside.

What we ship

  • AI that runs on a $80 Raspberry Pi
  • Creative tools for artists, not corporations
  • Education software that adapts to every kid
  • 100% open source. MIT license. Fork it.

The mission

  • Your data stays on your hardware
  • Smallest possible compute for every task
  • Solar-compatible, off-grid capable
  • If you can't run it yourself, it's not yours
